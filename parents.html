  • 100% in a sandboxed game world. No real systems are touched, ever.

What Village Hack is not

Common questions

Everything else parents ask.

What ages is this for?

Designed for 7-12 year olds who can read fluently. Younger kids may need a parent next to them. Teens who haven't done coding before will enjoy it too — the difficulty curve is real.

How long does it take to play through?

Season 1 takes most kids 1-3 hours total, usually spread across a few sittings. Season 2 is harder and takes 2-4 hours. New seasons ship regularly.

Does my kid need to know how to code already?

No. Village Hack starts from zero. By the end of Season 1, they'll understand binary, logic gates, encryption, and how databases work. By the end of Season 2, they'll be reading and reasoning about real code.

What does it cost?

Season 1 is free forever. No login, no card, no email required to play. Season 2 and future seasons require a subscription (pricing coming soon — sign up below to be notified).

Is it actually safe? My kid is young.

Completely. The whole game is a single-player browser experience with zero networking, zero chat, and zero ability to interact with anything outside the game. No data is collected from kids. We are committed to COPPA compliance.

What devices does it work on?

Any browser, any device. iPad, Chromebook, laptop, phone. No app to download. Best on a real keyboard but works on touch.

Who built this?

An engineer parent who got tired of finding either Fisher-Price coding apps or shady YouTube tutorials. Built for my own kid. Sharing it with yours.
